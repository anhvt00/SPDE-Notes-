SUMMARY

This lecture excerpt explores the transition from finite-dimensional calculus to the **mild approach** for solving **semi-linear stochastic partial differential equations** (SPDEs). To manage the mathematical complexity of **unbounded operators**, the author utilizes a decomposition strategy that isolates the dominant linear part of the equation within a **strongly continuous $C_0$ semigroup**. This framework effectively translates the problem into a **fixed-point problem** through a **variation of constants formula**, effectively turning the unbounded operator into a family of bounded linear operators. The text further illuminates these abstract concepts by detailing the **Laplace operator** on both the whole space and bounded domains, illustrating how **Dirichlet boundary conditions** and **Fourier expansions** define the domain of an **infinitesimal generator**. Ultimately, the purpose of the text is to provide the functional analytic foundation—specifically the **Hille-Yosida theorem**—necessary to represent complex evolution equations as manageable, integral-based systems.

Keywords: SPDE solution concepts, Semi-linear evolution equations, Mild solution approach, Strongly continuous semigroups, Infinitesimal generators
