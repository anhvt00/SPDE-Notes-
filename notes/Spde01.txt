SUMMARY

This lecture transcript introduces a seminar on **stochastic differential equations (SDEs)** and their sophisticated applications in **artificial intelligence** and statistical theory. The instructor begins by connecting mathematical theory to the modern technology of "stochastic derivative models," noting how **time reversal** in these equations enables the visual morphing effects seen in digital media. By examining the **empirical distribution** of independent Brownian motions, the source illustrates how microscopic particle movements converge into a macroscopic, deterministic **heat equation** as the number of particles approaches infinity. Furthermore, the text explores the transition from finite to **infinite-dimensional Gaussian measures**, emphasizing that the **trace of the covariance operator** must be finite for a valid probability measure to exist in these spaces. Ultimately, the course aims to provide a rigorous framework for understanding **stochastic fluctuations** and the long-term behavior of complex random systems.

Keywords: Stochastic differential equations, Particle distributions, Heat equation, Gaussian measures, Infinite dimensional space
